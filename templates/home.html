About Immigration

The immigration data set used can be found here
The data shows the number of people granted lawful permanent residence(in 2008) in the form of;

Legal Permanent Resident: to become a permanent resident, an applicant must apply for a green card, once granted a Permanent Resident card(Green Card), one can work and live in the US permanently. More info about process and application found at Green Card - USCIS.

Tourist: through the Visa waiver program, a tourist can apply for B-2 Tourist Visa by going to ESTA webpage.

Worker: a worker must obtain a Temporary(Nonimmigrant) Visa in order to enter the US, there are variouse types of worker Visas, more info can be found at this USCIS webpage.

Student: to obtain a student Visa, a student can fill out a DS-160 form by applying here. More info found at Travel State Gov webpage.

Asylum/Refugee: do you feel persecuted? Have you been already? Then apply for refugee status today! Apply Now!

Naturalized: to become a naturalized citizen there are certain requirements that need to be meet, having a Green Card for five years for instance, but once naturalized, a permanent resident gives up allegiance to any other nation and is a citizen of the United States. More info at Naturalization Info.
